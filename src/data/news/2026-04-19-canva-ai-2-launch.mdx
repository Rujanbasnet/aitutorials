Canva just shipped the biggest update in its 13-year history. [Canva AI 2.0](https://www.canva.com/newsroom/news/canva-create-2026-ai/), announced at the company's Create 2026 event in Los Angeles, transforms the design platform from a template picker into a full conversational design system — one that remembers your preferences, connects to your work tools, and executes multi-step projects on its own.

## What Changed

The centrepiece is **conversational design**. Instead of browsing templates and dragging elements, you describe what you need — "a LinkedIn carousel summarising our Q1 results" or "an Instagram story announcing our sale" — and Canva builds it. The result is fully editable, not a static image, so you can still fine-tune every element.

Behind the scenes, Canva added what it calls **Agentic Orchestration** — a layer that coordinates the platform's tools to handle multi-step tasks. Give it a campaign brief and it can generate social posts, presentation slides, and email headers in one go, each formatted for the right platform.

The third major addition is **Living Memory**. Canva now learns your brand colours, fonts, tone, and layout preferences over time and applies them automatically. If you always use the same logo placement or colour palette, the AI picks that up without you repeating yourself.

## How It Connects to Your Work

New connectors link Canva to Slack, Notion, Zoom, Gmail, Google Drive, and Google Calendar. The AI can pull context from a meeting transcript or email thread and turn it into a briefing document or presentation — no copy-pasting required.

## What This Means for You

If you use Canva for social media, presentations, or marketing materials, AI 2.0 removes most of the manual setup. Describe the outcome you want and the platform handles the execution. It is rolling out now as a research preview to the first million users, with broader access coming in the following weeks.
